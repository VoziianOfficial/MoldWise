What Cookies Are

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by a website. They can help a website remember preferences, keep features working, understand how visitors interact with pages, and support security or spam-prevention features.

Similar technologies may include local storage, session storage, pixels, tags, scripts, or analytics tools.

Types of Cookies We May Use

  • Essential cookies: These help the website function, remember cookie choices, support navigation, and keep basic features working.
  • Preference cookies: These may remember choices such as consent status or interface preferences.
  • Analytics cookies: These may help understand general website usage, traffic patterns, page performance, and visitor interactions.
  • Security or spam-prevention technologies: These may help protect forms, reduce abuse, and keep the website reliable.
  • Third-party cookies: Third-party tools, embedded scripts, fonts, analytics services, or other integrations may use their own technologies.

Cookie Consent

This website includes a cookie consent banner with accept and cancel choices. The banner is designed to remain visible until you choose an action. Once you make a choice, that choice may be stored locally so the banner does not keep appearing on every page load.

If you clear browser data, use another browser, use another device, or block local storage, the banner may appear again.

Your Cookie Controls

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to block, delete, or limit cookies. You can also clear local storage or site data through browser privacy settings.

Blocking cookies may affect certain website features, including consent memory, navigation behavior, analytics accuracy, or form-related protections.
